St. Thérèse of Lisieux (1873-1897) was a Carmelite nun who became a popular saint for her simple and practical approach to spirituality. She is also known as the "Little Flower"

Life:

  • Born Marie-Françoise-Thérèse Martin in Alençon, France
  • Her mother died of breast cancer in 1877
  • Joined the Carmelite convent in Lisieux at age 15
  • Died of tuberculosis at age 24

Legacy:

  • Wrote the spiritual memoir The Story of a Soul
  • Became a doctor of the church in 1997
  • Patron saint of missionaries, florists, pilots, priests, and the sick
  • Her message of small sacrifices and intentional acts of love continues to inspire people today

Teachings:

  • Believed in the "Little Way" of doing good on earth through small sacrifices
  • Promised to spend her heaven doing good on earth
  • Believed that God's mercy would make her the saint he created her to be
